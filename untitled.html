Why are you reading this?/Why software engineering?




Spending my last £2000 on career coaching after years of feeling
unfulfilled and unchallenged at work, I was left with the gut-wrenching belief that
finding a motivating, enjoyable career was out of reach for me. Unfortunately,
skills I enjoyed using were not important in the careers available to me.

Down £2000, anxious, and with nothing left to lose I
enrolled on a computer science course (CS50 from HarvardX). Whilst working on
the second problem set, I remember feeling alive and energised as I tried to solve
an equation for printing a series of hash symbols in C. I hadn’t felt this
feeling of satisfaction and accomplishment for a long time and it was in stark
contrast to how I felt about my job.

Since then, over the past one and a half years I have been
learning different aspects of software development, implementing them in
projects whilst working as a teacher/tutor. Throughout this process, I have
been dealing with self-doubt and frustration but also perseverance, reward, and
joy.

Going through this process, as challenging as it was, has evolved
me, and I am confident in my abilities to learn, adapt and use my skills to
benefit others, in ways that are empowering and exciting for me. I am sure this
makes me a strong, positive contributor to any team that I am on.
