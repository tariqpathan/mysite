About Me

I've been teaching mostly chemistry and physics GCSE/A-Level over the past 6 years. Graduated with a distinction in Biochemical Engineering (MSc)

Why Software Engineering?

Spending my last £2000 on career coaching after years of feeling unfulfilled and unchallenged at work, I was left with the gut-wrenching belief that finding a motivating, enjoyable career was out of reach for me.

Down £2000 and frustrated with my situation, I decided to take a risk and enrolled on a computer science course (CS50x from Harvard). Working on the second problem set, I remember feeling alive and energised as I tried to solve an equation for printing a series of hash symbols in C. I hadn’t felt this feeling of satisfaction and accomplishment for a long time and it was in stark contrast to how I felt about my job.

Since then, over the past one and a half years I have been learning different aspects of software development, implementing them in projects whilst working as a teacher/tutor. Throughout this process, I have been dealing with self-doubt and frustration but this has been outweighed by the reward and joy from persevering through.

Going through this process, as challenging as it was, has evolved me, and I am confident in my abilities to learn, adapt and use my skills to benefit others, in ways that are empowering and exciting for me. I am sure this makes me a strong, positive contributor to any team that I am on.

Personal Values

Coding, for me, is the art of Googling a query 10 different ways in 20 tabs!

I flourish in independent environments built on trust. Accountable, respectful, and reliable – I firmly believe the way you treat others reflects on yourself.

Energised by tasks involving planning, co-ordination and strong problem-solving aspects. Communicating complex concepts is rewarding, helping clarify my understanding whilst challenging me with different viewpoints.

Given my education and employment background, I am curious about the intersection of technology with areas such as medicine, education, mental health, and philosophy. Looking for opportunities where growth is prioritised and mistakes are treated as learning opportunities.
